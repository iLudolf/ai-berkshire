# First-Class Companies (Other Picks): Quality Elimination Screen

**Screening Date**: May 17, 2026
**Screening Criteria**: 7 quality elimination metrics + 2 exemption rules (v2)

---

## Metric Framework

| # | Metric | Elimination Condition |
|---|--------|-----------------------|
| 1 | 10-year average ROE | < 8% |
| 2 | 5-year cumulative free cash flow | Negative |
| 3 | Interest coverage ratio | < 2x |
| 4 | Long-term gross margin | < 15% |
| 5 | Operating cash flow / net income (5-year avg) | < 0.7 |
| 6 | Long-term net margin | < 5% |
| 7 | 5-year share dilution | > 20% |

Exemption A (Metric 1): Listed < 10 years + gross margin > 30% + positive operating cash flow in recent 2 years
Exemption B (Metric 6): Gross margin > 30% + net margin > 5% in recent 2 years or on an upward trend

## Detailed Analysis by Company

### 1. Costco (COST) — Membership Retail

| Metric | Value | Judgment |
|--------|-------|----------|
| ①10-year average ROE | ~26% (10-year avg 25.9%) | ✅ Well above 8% |
| ②5-year cumulative FCF | $6.6B (2024) + $7.8B (2025) + positive in prior years, 5-year cumulative ~$30B+ | ✅ |
| ③Interest coverage ratio | 71x (FY2025) | ✅ Well above 2x |
| ④Long-term gross margin | **12–13%** | ❌ Below 15% |
| ⑤OCF/NI (5-year avg) | ~1.5x (FY2025: 13.3B/8.1B = 1.65) | ✅ |
| ⑥Long-term net margin | **2.5–3.0%** | ❌ Below 5% |
| ⑦5-year share dilution | ~0% (~443M shares essentially unchanged) | ✅ |

**Conclusion: Failed (Metric 4 gross margin, Metric 6 net margin)**

**Special Note**: Costco is the classic example of a membership business model. Its merchandise is priced on a "no profit" principle (gross margin is intentionally kept at 12–13%), with profit coming from membership fee revenue. The double-low gross margin and net margin are therefore not a signal of weak competitiveness, but a deliberate result of the business model design. However, under the hard standards of this metric framework, it cannot pass Metrics 4 and 6, and a gross margin below 30% does not satisfy Exemption B conditions.

| ⑥Long-term net margin | TTM 9.6%, 2023: 11.4%, but 2022: only 1.3%, early years negative | ⚠️→✅ Exemption B (gross margin > 30% + net margin > 5% in recent 2 years and on upward trend) |
| ⑦5-year share dilution | ~0.5%/year increase, ~2–3% over 5 years (from ~310M to ~321M shares) | ✅ |

**Exemption A conditions met**: Listed < 10 years (IPO 2019, only 7 years) ✅ + gross margin > 30% (61%) ✅ + positive OCF in recent 2 years (2023: ¥2.9B, 2024: ¥4.2B) ✅

**Exemption B conditions met**: Gross margin > 30% (61%) ✅ + net margin > 5% in recent 2 years (2023: 11.4%, 2024 TTM: 9.6%) ✅ + upward trend ✅

**Conclusion: Conditionally Passed (dependent on Exemption A + B)**

**Caveats**: Luckin's 5-year cumulative FCF includes negative periods in 2022, but over the 2021–2025 horizon ($0.12B + -$0.11B + $0.02B + positive + positive), the total is positive — barely passing. However, Luckin has a short history, a past accounting fraud scandal, and operates in a fiercely competitive restaurant/coffee industry. Passing the quality elimination screen does not mean it is a good investment target; additional in-depth research is required.

### Failed (1 Company)

| Company | Reason for Failure |
|---------|--------------------|
| Costco | Metric 4: gross margin 12–13% < 15%; Metric 6: net margin 2.5–3% < 5% |

**Explanation for Costco's failure**: The membership retail business model deliberately suppresses merchandise markups, with profit sourced from membership fees rather than product margins. The double-low gross margin and net margin are a consequence of model design, not insufficient competitiveness. Costco is an excellent company, but the hard standards of this metric framework cannot accommodate "low-margin, high-turnover" business models. Investors may view Costco as a case study in the limitations of the metric framework — a great company does not necessarily pass every quantitative screen.
