# Quality Elimination Screen Stress Test Report

> Test Date: 2026-05-17
> Objective: Verify whether the 7 quality elimination indicators can accurately distinguish good companies from poor companies

---

## Review of the 7 Quality Elimination Indicators

| # | Indicator | Elimination Condition |
|---|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | 10-year average ROE | < 8% |
| 2 | 5-year cumulative free cash flow | Negative |
| 3 | Interest coverage ratio (EBIT/Interest) | < 2x |
| 4 | Long-term gross margin | < 15% |
| 5 | Operating cash flow / Net income (5-year average) | < 0.7 |
| 6 | Long-term net profit margin | < 5% |
| 7 | 5-year total share count expansion | > 20% (excluding M&A reasons) |

Exemption A (applies to Indicator 1): Listed fewer than 10 years + gross margin >30% + positive operating cash flow in last 2 years → eligible for exemption
Exemption B (applies to Indicator 6): Gross margin >30% + net profit margin already >5% or on an upward trend in last 2 years → eligible for exemption

### Key Findings

#### 1. Ability to Eliminate Poor Companies: Excellent

- All 23 poor/bad companies were **eliminated** (elimination rate 100%)
- The fewest violations was 4 indicators (Valeant), with most violating 5–7
- No companies slipped through the net

#### 2. "False Negatives" Analysis: None

Among the 23 definitively poor companies, not a single one escaped the 7-indicator screen. Even fraudulent companies (such as Kangmei Pharmaceutical and Huishan Dairy), although their reported gross margins passed on the surface, other indicators (particularly OCF/NI and FCF) still exposed the problems.

**Conclusion: The 7 indicators achieved a 100% identification rate for poor companies, with zero false negatives.**

#### 3. "False Positives" Analysis: Present but Acceptable

11 "mediocre companies" in Part 3 were eliminated (55% elimination rate). Analysis of the rationale:

**Justified eliminations (not false positives):**
- China Metallurgical (ROE 2.5%, gross margin 10%, net margin 1–2%): genuinely not worth investing in
- Shougang Corp (ROE 3%, gross margin 5%): genuinely not worth investing in
- Air China (cyclical losses, high leverage ~90%): genuinely high risk
- Vanke A (near blow-up in 2024): risk was identified in advance
- BOE Technology (massive capex, continuous dilutive financing): genuinely "value-destroying growth"
- Walgreens (already heading toward bankruptcy): actually already a poor company

**Borderline cases (debatable):**
- Aluminum Corp of China (only violated ROE and net margin, cyclical reasons): issue specific to cyclical stocks
- China Unicom (ROE 6%, net margin 2%): genuinely mediocre, elimination makes sense
- China Communications Construction / China State Construction (state infrastructure enterprise characteristics): low gross margin is an industry attribute
- SAIC Motor (once excellent, now declining): declining trend was identified in advance
