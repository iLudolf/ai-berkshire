## 1. Yangtze Power (600900.SH)

**Market cap**: ~CNY 640bn | **PE(TTM)**: ~19.5x | **Dividend yield**: ~3.6%

### Core Investment Thesis

Exclusive operator of six world-class cascade hydropower stations along the main stem of the Yangtze River (Three Gorges, Gezhouba, Xiluodu, Xiangjiaba, Wudongde, Baihetan) — "one drop of water generates electricity six times" through six-reservoir joint dispatch, creating highly certain cash flows. In a low-rate environment, high dividends (committed at no less than 70%) + depreciation release (Three Gorges unit depreciation rolling to maturity) produce long-term profit growth certainty.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Supply-side resource monopoly is the ultimate moat — Yangtze main stem hydropower resources are non-renewable; development rights granted by the state and already entirely consolidated into the company; six-reservoir joint dispatch creates ~60–70bn kWh of additional annual output through synergies; policy barriers are extremely high, with no new entrant possible.

### Valuation Assessment

Reasonable. PE 19.5x is at the low end of the 3-year historical range, significantly below the power sector average of 29.7x; 3.6% dividend yield is highly attractive in a low-rate environment.

## 2. China Shenhua Energy (601088.SH)

**Market cap**: ~CNY 1,000bn | **PE(TTM)**: ~15–16x | **Dividend yield**: ~6%

### Core Investment Thesis

Integrated coal-power-transport value chain creates internal synergy; anti-cyclical resilience far exceeds pure coal companies. Following completion of the billion-level asset restructuring in 2025, resource reserves and earnings power are further enhanced. High dividend yield (~6%) + low valuation provides a strong margin of safety.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Integrated value chain (coal mines + power plants + railways + ports + shipping) creates a systemic barrier that no single-segment competitor can replicate; #1 coal resource reserves domestically; own transport network reduces costs and ensures supply stability.

### Valuation Assessment

Cheap-to-fair. Corresponding to 2026E earnings, PE is only 7–8x; combined with 6% dividend yield, margin of safety is ample.

## 3. Zijin Mining (601899.SH)

**Market cap**: ~CNY 1,000bn | **PE(TTM)**: ~14–15x

### Core Investment Thesis

Global mining expansion strategy with exceptional execution; gold-copper dual engines benefit from global inflation expectations and energy transition trends. Production volume continues growing strongly (2025–2027E copper and gold volume CAGR 15%+); evolved from a "cyclical stock" to a "resource + growth" dual-engine holding.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Moat is relatively limited — mining is fundamentally a competition in resource acquisition capability + operational efficiency. Zijin's advantage lies in management's extremely strong project acquisition and mine operation capability (technically leading in low-grade ore development), but it does not possess a monopolistic resource barrier; copper and gold prices remain the core variable.

### Valuation Assessment

Reasonable. 14–15x PE corresponding to a high-growth phase is fair-to-cheap, but mining stock cyclicality and gold/copper price volatility risk must be considered.

## 4. Shaanxi Coal Industry (601225.SH)

**Market cap**: ~CNY 220bn | **PE(TTM)**: ~13x | **Dividend yield**: ~5%

### Core Investment Thesis

Outstanding resource endowment of northern Shaanxi high-quality coal mines (among the lowest per-tonne cost in the industry); stable high-dividend policy (dividend yield ~5%); coal price centre has retreated but earnings resilience is strong. 2026E profit recovery growth (estimated CNY 19.6bn, +17%).

### Economic Moat Analysis

Resource endowment is the core advantage — northern Shaanxi mining district has thick seams, shallow burial, and low extraction costs. However, moat width is inferior to China Shenhua's integrated model; at its core this is still a single coal mining company, meaningfully exposed to coal price cycles.

### Valuation Assessment

Reasonable. 13x PE is in the reasonable range for the coal sector; 5% dividend yield provides a cushion.

## 5. Huaneng Hydropower (600025.SH)

**Market cap**: ~CNY 170bn | **PE(TTM)**: ~19–20x

### Core Investment Thesis

Holds the Lancang River basin hydropower development rights — the second-largest hydropower operator after Yangtze Power. New energy plants coming onstream open a second growth curve; wind-solar-hydro-storage integrated development adds to long-term earnings.

## 7. SDIC Power (600886.SH)

**Market cap**: ~CNY 120bn | **PE(TTM)**: ~16–17x

### Core Investment Thesis

Core asset is the controlling interest in Yalong River Hydropower (China's third-largest hydropower basin); immense development potential in wind-solar-hydro-storage integration. Yalong River basin planned installed capacity exceeds 60mn kW; less than half developed so far — long-term growth path is clear.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Exclusive rights to Yalong River basin development create the core moat — same resource monopoly logic as Yangtze Power and Huaneng Hydropower. However, the company also has thermal power assets weighing on overall asset quality.

### Valuation Assessment

Fair-to-cheap. 16–17x PE is at historical lows; Yalong River's long-term development value is not fully priced.
