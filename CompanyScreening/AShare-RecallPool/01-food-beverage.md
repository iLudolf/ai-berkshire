## 1. Kweichow Moutai (600519)

**Market cap**: CNY 1,670bn | **PE(TTM)**: 20x | **Dividend yield**: 3.9%

### Core Investment Thesis

Absolute king of sauce-aroma baijiu; brand mindshare in Chinese consumer goods is unrivalled. Feitian Moutai functions as both a consumer product and a social currency. Naturally constrained production capacity (five-year brewing cycle) combined with absolute pricing power creates one of the very few consumer monopolies where "volume and price can still rise together." Even after revenue and net income both declined for the first time in 2025, the company still earned CNY 82.3bn in a single year — profit resilience far exceeds peers.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Brand moat has reached "cultural icon" status; Feitian Moutai's position in business banquets and gift-giving cannot be displaced in the near term. Production scarcity creates a natural supply constraint — the core production zone in Maotai-zhen, the five-year brewing cycle, and the base-liquor reserve system are impossible for competitors to replicate. Pricing power is extreme: the gap between ex-factory price and market price has persisted for years, and the company possesses the rare ability to raise prices without losing volume. The dealer network is undergoing adjustment, but channel control remains the strongest in the industry. The only concerns are policy risk and generational consumption shifts, though no substantial threat is visible in the short-to-medium term.

### Valuation Assessment

A 20x PE on CNY 82.3bn net income sits at the low end of the historical valuation range. A 3.9% dividend yield is attractive for a business of this quality. Given the industry may be at a cyclical trough, the current valuation is "fair-to-cheap" — any meaningful pullback would represent a rare buying window.

**Conclusion**: The closest asset to a "perpetual money-printing machine" in Chinese consumer goods. Worth holding a full position when valuation is reasonable.

## 2. Wuliangye (000858)

**Market cap**: CNY 358bn | **PE(TTM)**: 11x | **Dividend yield**: 4.5%

### Core Investment Thesis

Absolute leader in strong-aroma baijiu, with brand power second only to Moutai, holding a solid "number two" position in banquet and business settings. The combination of 11x PE + 4.5% dividend yield is highly attractive; however, the 2025 accounting-error adjustment incident exposed management's shortcomings in financial transparency — first-rate business, discounted management.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Brand moat is strong; Wuliangye's mindshare in strong-aroma baijiu has been built over decades and cannot be shaken in the short term. The ancient cellar pool resources (Ming Dynasty historic cellars) constitute a physically scarce asset. The nationwide channel network is mature, with broad dealer coverage. Scale advantages deliver competitive cost leadership in grain procurement and brewing efficiency. However, the brand has a history of over-licensing that diluted brand purity; although significantly curtailed in recent years, brand purity still trails Moutai.

### Valuation Assessment

11x PE is in clearly undervalued territory among tier-one baijiu companies; 4.5% dividend yield provides a cushion. The low valuation partly reflects the market's punishment for the accounting-error event and the discount applied to management credibility. If management rebuilds trust, there is meaningful re-rating potential.

## 3. Luzhou Laojiao (000568)

**Market cap**: CNY 144bn | **PE(TTM)**: 16x | **Dividend yield**: 3.6%

### Core Investment Thesis

Guojiao 1573 is highly competitive in the sub-premium to premium price tier, with deep brand heritage ("originator of strong-aroma baijiu" positioning). Revenue and net income both declined for the first time in 2025, but this reflects a cyclical industry adjustment; brand equity has not been materially impaired. As a "wounded champion" in an industry downturn, it offers upside leverage when the cycle turns.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Possesses scarce brewing resources including the 1573 National Treasure Cellar Pool complex; the "originator of strong-aroma baijiu" brand positioning carries deep historical resonance. Guojiao 1573 has established clear brand recognition in the CNY 800–1,500 price band. Management performance over the past decade has been outstanding — returning the company from an industry laggard to a tier-one player. Relative to Moutai and Wuliangye, brand momentum still trails; in extreme industry downturns, the impact is correspondingly larger. Channel management is among the best in the industry, and inventory control is comparatively disciplined.

### Valuation Assessment

16x PE is in a reasonable range; 3.6% dividend yield provides a baseline return. Given 2025 represents an earnings trough, the effective PE may be overstated (PE at a trough is elevated). The key question is whether 2026 earnings can stabilise and recover; if the industry rebounds, current prices offer meaningful upside leverage.

## 4. Shanxi Fenjiu (600809)

**Market cap**: CNY 221.6bn | **PE(TTM)**: 18x | **Dividend yield**: 4.0%

### Core Investment Thesis

Absolute leader in light-aroma baijiu, with a Moutai-like position within its flavour category. Nationwide expansion is accelerating (out-of-province revenue already at 65%), making it one of the few tier-one baijiu brands still "expanding territory." Maintained positive growth through the 2025 industry downturn, demonstrating counter-cyclical resilience.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Virtually no comparable competitor in the light-aroma baijiu category; the Qinghua Fenjiu series covers the CNY 300–1,500 price band comprehensively. Brand history traceable to 6,000 years ago in Xinghuacun, with deep cultural assets. Nationwide strategy has been well executed; out-of-province markets still offer penetration room (compared with Moutai's and Wuliangye's nationally balanced distribution). Light-aroma's clean, refreshing taste is relatively more accepted by younger consumers, offering potential advantages in generational consumption transitions. Risk: growth may slow in later stages of national expansion, and the ceiling for light-aroma at the ultra-premium (CNY 2,000+) tier has yet to be proven.

### Valuation Assessment

18x PE paired with positive earnings growth is moderate among tier-one baijiu. The 4.0% dividend yield provides a cushion. Compared with Luzhou Laojiao's declining earnings, Fenjiu's growth visibility is stronger, and the valuation premium has fundamental support. Fair, not cheap.

## 5. Yanghe Brewery (002304)

**Market cap**: CNY 105bn | **PE(TTM)**: 37x (depressed base) | **Dividend yield**: 10%

### Core Investment Thesis

Representative of mellow-style baijiu; the Dream Blue series has meaningful brand equity in the sub-premium segment. However, the 62–68% crash in 2025 net income exposed severe channel inventory overhangs and strategic execution failures by management. The 10% dividend yield looks attractive but its sustainability is in doubt. Currently in a phase of "acceptable business model but deteriorating operating condition."

### Economic Moat Analysis

The "mellow style" category positioning offers some differentiation but falls far short of the category exclusivity commanded by light-aroma Fenjiu. Dream Blue M6+/M9 has brand recognition in sub-premium, but faces strong competition from Guojiao 1573 and Qinghua Fenjiu. Channel depth in its Jiangsu home base gives it high provincial market share, though national expansion has underperformed expectations. The product matrix (Sea Blue / Sky Blue / Dream Blue) is broadly covered but causes brand dilution. The most significant concern is that management's execution capability is clearly inferior to peers such as Luzhou Laojiao and Fenjiu.

### Valuation Assessment

37x PE is "artificially inflated" by a depressed base; normalised PE would likely be in the 15–18x range. The 10% dividend yield reflects a payout policy from prior boom years; future dividend capacity depends on earnings recovery. Current valuation is not expensive, but management risk means it "looks cheap but is not cheap."

## 6. Haitian Flavouring (603288)

**Market cap**: CNY 218.2bn | **PE(TTM)**: 31x | **Dividend yield**: 3.0%

### Core Investment Thesis

Absolute leader in the condiment industry, with a dominant soy sauce market share far ahead of number two. The triple combination of brand recognition + channel penetration + scale cost advantages forms an extremely high competitive barrier. Growth is steady but valuation is rich — a classic "great company, wait for a fair price" situation.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Brand moat in condiments is unmatched — "Haitian" is practically synonymous with soy sauce in consumers' minds. The channel network covers more than one million terminals nationwide, including county- and township-level markets, and the distribution density is virtually impossible to replicate. Scale-driven cost leadership enables decisive competitive responses on pricing. Condiment purchases are high frequency, brand loyalty is strong, and while switching costs are low, the stickiness of habit is substantial. In recent years the company has extended into compound seasonings, oyster sauce, and cooking wine — its platform capability is being validated.

### Valuation Assessment

31x PE corresponds to solid but not high-growth earnings (revenue growth 10–15%); PEG is in the 2–2.5x range, making the valuation expensive. A 3.0% dividend yield is unremarkable. This is a company worth long-term tracking and waiting for a "fair price" — the current price does not offer sufficient value. If PE fell below 25x, attractiveness would increase significantly.

## 7. Yili Group (600887)

**Market cap**: CNY 173.1bn | **PE(TTM)**: 15x | **Dividend yield**: 6.0%

### Core Investment Thesis

Absolute leader in Chinese dairy, with 2025 net income of CNY 11.6bn (+37%) reaching a historical high and ROE of 21% demonstrating first-rate capital returns. A 15x PE + 6% dividend yield is exceptionally rare among consumer leaders, making it one of the best "certainty + valuation" combinations in the A-share food and beverage sector today.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Scale advantages are pronounced across four value chain nodes: upstream milk source control (upstream pasture network), processing capacity, cold-chain logistics, and channel coverage — all industry-leading. Brand power in dairy sits in the top tier alongside Mengniu, but Yili has consistently led on profit margin and operating efficiency. Product matrix covers liquid milk, infant formula, ice cream, and cheese. Purchase frequency is extremely high (daily necessities), with stable demand. Dual-oligopoly structure continues to drive industry concentration. Risk: overall dairy growth is slowing, and raw-milk price cycle fluctuations affect short-term profits.

### Valuation Assessment

15x PE versus CNY 11.6bn net income and 21% ROE places valuation in clearly undervalued territory. A 6% dividend yield is highly attractive among blue-chip large caps — essentially a "bond-like income + growth" combination. Even if future growth slows to low single digits, the current valuation still delivers satisfactory returns. This is a "buy now, don't wait" valuation level.

## 8. Dongpeng Beverage (605499)

**Market cap**: CNY 139.6bn | **PE(TTM)**: 25x | **Dividend yield**: 2.8%

### Core Investment Thesis

High-growth leader in China's functional beverage segment, with 2025 revenue growth of 31% demonstrating strong momentum. PEG < 1 suggests the market has not fully priced in its growth rate. Second-curve initiative (electrolyte water "Bushui La" etc.) is clearly articulated — evolving from a "single-hero-product company" into a "beverage platform."

### Economic Moat Analysis

In the functional beverage segment, the company has established the mental position of "blue-collar functional beverage #1" through a value-for-money strategy and channel penetration in lower-tier cities. At the CNY 3–5 price point, there are virtually no competitors with comparable brand equity. Channel network is expanding nationally from its Guangdong base — national coverage is still in progress with substantial incremental potential. The flagship product "Dongpeng Special Drink" is proven; the fast ramp-up of new product "Bushui La" validates platform capability. Risks: Is the functional beverage segment ceiling high enough? If Red Bull resolves its trademark dispute, could it mount a counter-offensive?

### Valuation Assessment

25x PE versus 30%+ revenue growth gives a PEG of approximately 0.8 — reasonable-to-cheap for a high-growth consumer stock. At 2.8%, the dividend yield is generous for a growth stock. If 25–30% growth can be sustained for two or more years, the current valuation is clearly attractive. The key variable is growth durability.

## 9. Chongqing Beer (600132)

**Market cap**: CNY 27.1bn | **PE(TTM)**: 21x | **Dividend yield**: 4.5%

### Core Investment Thesis

Carlsberg's China platform with a clear premiumisation strategy (multi-brand matrix: Wusu, 1664, Carlsberg, etc.). Holds a monopolistic position in Western China regional markets. A 98% payout ratio demonstrates the foreign-controlled company's characteristic of "all profit = dividends," but growth momentum is insufficient.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Regional monopoly advantage is clear — very high market share in core markets such as Chongqing and Xinjiang, making it difficult for competitors to break through. Carlsberg's global brand matrix (Wusu + 1664 + Carlsberg + Lefort) covers different price tiers and consumption occasions. Premiumisation trend supports per-keg price growth. However, the beer industry as a whole is entering a volume-saturation era with little total volume growth — progress can only come from structural upgrading. Wusu's national rollout has begun showing signs of slowing growth, and brand premium may be diluted once it moves beyond its home turf.

### Valuation Assessment

21x PE paired with low single-digit growth is not cheap. A 4.5% dividend yield (98% payout ratio) essentially treats the company as a "dividend machine." If growth remains sluggish, 21x PE offers limited upside leverage. Better suited to defensive investors seeking stable cash flow than those seeking capital appreciation.
