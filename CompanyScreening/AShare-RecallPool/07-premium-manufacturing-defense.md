## 1. Sany Heavy Industry (600031.SH)

**Market cap**: ~CNY 187bn | **PE(TTM)**: ~18–22x | **P/B**: ~2.4x

### Core Investment Thesis

Global construction machinery leader; #1 global excavator market share. Overseas revenue exceeds 50% — a genuine leap from "China leader" to "global leader." Driven by dual engines: digital-intelligent transformation (Roots Industrial Internet platform) + global expansion. Construction machinery is at a cyclical trough; domestic utilisation rates are recovering from lows; overseas markets continue to grow.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Brand + channel + service network form global barriers. Sany has a comprehensive global dealer and service network; once customers use Sany equipment, after-sales service and spare parts supply create a lock-in effect. Digital-intelligent capabilities (remote diagnostics, predictive maintenance) further deepen customer stickiness. Scale effects provide cost advantages. On the other hand, construction machinery is meaningfully cyclical; Caterpillar, Komatsu, and other global giants still have technical advantages in the high-end segment; Sany's profit margin still trails international leaders.

### Valuation Assessment

Fair-to-cheap at cyclical trough. A 18–22x PE appears elevated but this is the industry's earnings trough — normalised earnings PE could be 12–15x. A P/B of 2.4x against 15%+ normalised ROE is attractive. The cyclical trough in construction machinery is typically the best buying opportunity.

## 2. AVIC Jonhon Optronic (002179.SZ)

**Market cap**: ~CNY 78.5bn | **PE(TTM)**: ~28–35x

### Core Investment Thesis

Absolute leader in military connectors with 40%+ defence market share. Connectors are the "nervous system" of weapons systems; each successive generation of equipment requires higher-density, higher-reliability connectors. Technical barriers + military certification barriers are extremely high; new entrants require 5–8 years to pass all qualification certifications. Civil sectors (communications, NEVs) are opening a second growth curve.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Dual barriers form an extremely strong moat. Technical barriers: high-reliability connectors require deep material, process, and design expertise; failure costs are extreme (equipment failure can result in loss of life). Certification barriers: military suppliers must pass rigorous classified qualifications, quality management certifications, and airborne/platform verification — multi-year cycles; once qualified, very difficult to replace. On the other hand, the defence industry is constrained by defence budget, limiting the growth ceiling; 2023–2025 saw an order adjustment period with significant earnings volatility.

### Valuation Assessment

Expensive but worth waiting for. PE 28–35x is at the historical middle-to-high range, corresponding to current profit (which is depressed by order timing). If 2026–2027 defence orders recover and profit returns to normal, forward PE could drop to the 20–25x range. Great sector, great company — but need to wait for profit recovery to a reasonable level before assessing value.

## 3. Guoguang Semiconductor (002049.SZ)

**Market cap**: ~CNY 59bn | **PE(2026E)**: ~38x

### Core Investment Thesis

Leader in specialised chips (military-grade FPGA, secure chips); extremely high certification barriers. The specialised chip market has triple entry barriers (classified qualification + military certification + technical accumulation); once a product passes platform verification, replacement probability is extremely low. Gross margin exceeds 60%; net margin exceeds 40% — profitability is top-tier among A-share chip companies.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Certification barriers + technical barriers form an extremely strong moat. Only 2–3 domestic suppliers of specialised FPGA; Guoguang leads in market share. Secure chip (SIM card, bank card, ID card chip) global market share is top three. On the other hand, earnings are volatile — defence orders have a "feast or famine" pattern; 2024–2025 is in an order trough with significantly declining profit; and the total addressable specialised chip market is limited (tens of billions of CNY scale), with a visible growth ceiling.

### Valuation Assessment

Expensive. PE(2026E) of 38x reflects a trough-recovery expectation. If profit recovery misses, valuation will face pressure. Given earnings volatility and growth ceiling, the current valuation lacks sufficient margin of safety.

## 4. Leader Harmonics (688017.SH)

**Market cap**: ~CNY 39bn | **PE(TTM)**: ~429x

### Core Investment Thesis

Domestic harmonic reducer leader; broke Japan Harmonic Drive Systems' long-term monopoly. Harmonic reducers are core components in industrial robots and collaborative robots (accounting for 10–15% of robot cost); high technical barriers and substantial domestic substitution potential. The humanoid robot narrative provides valuation upside optionality.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Technical barriers exist but are not insurmountable. Leader Harmonics has approached Harmonic Drive Systems' precision and longevity metrics, with a cost advantage. However, domestic competitors such as Laige and Shuanghuan are catching up rapidly; harmonic reducers are not a "winner-takes-all" market. Traditional industrial robot growth is slowing; large-scale humanoid robot production is at least 3–5 years away — near-term profit support is lacking.

### Valuation Assessment

Severely overvalued. A 429x PE means the current share price is entirely driven by long-range humanoid robot expectations rather than fundamentals. Even using the most optimistic 2027E profit, PE would still be 80–100x. This is a "dream-priced" rather than "value-priced" holding. For value investors, the current valuation offers zero margin of safety.
