## 1. China Merchants Bank (600036.SH)

**Market cap**: ~CNY 1,010bn | **PE(TTM)**: ~6.3x | **Dividend yield**: ~5%

### Core Investment Thesis

Benchmark retail bank; ROE consistently above 13%; NPL ratio of only 0.95%; provision coverage ratio of 412% — asset quality leads listed banks by a wide margin. CMB's core capabilities are low-cost liabilities (high current deposit mix) and retail client management (AUM exceeds CNY 14tn), which create a sustained net interest margin advantage. In a falling rate environment, CMB's liability-side advantage will compress, but relative advantage remains prominent.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Brand + retail client base form the deepest bank moat. CMB's brand mindshare in retail finance is extremely strong; high-net-worth client stickiness is high and migration costs are large; Sunflower and above clients contribute 80%+ of AUM, and private banking assets under management rank first in the industry. Technology investment has exceeded CNY 10bn annually for multiple consecutive years; digital capabilities lead peers. On the other hand, the banking sector as a whole faces pressure from net interest margin compression and slowing profit growth — CMB is no exception.

### Valuation Assessment

Cheap. PE 6.3x, P/B ~1.0x, at the lower range of history. Given ROE of 13%+ in profitability terms, the current valuation provides ample margin of safety. A 5% dividend yield provides a floor for holding.

## 2. Ping An Insurance (601318.SH)

**Market cap**: ~CNY 1,100bn | **PE(TTM)**: ~7–8x | **P/EV**: ~0.64x

### Core Investment Thesis

Integrated financial leader; life insurance reform entering the harvest phase. In 2023–2025, the agent force was cleansed (reduced from millions to ~400,000 elite agents), dramatically improving per-agent productivity. NBV (new business value) has returned to positive growth for consecutive periods — the life insurance fundamental inflection point is confirmed. A P/EV of 0.64x means the market assigns almost zero value to any future new business — margin of safety is extremely abundant.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Comprehensive financial licences create a scarcity barrier — full licence coverage across life insurance + P&C insurance + banking + trust + securities + technology, with a unique cross-selling capability. Brand recognition in insurance ranks first. On the other hand, property investment exposure (real estate + real estate-related credit) remains the market's biggest concern; although substantial impairments have been recognised, tail risk is not fully eliminated.

### Valuation Assessment

Extremely cheap. P/EV of 0.64x is at historical lows, implying the assumption that Ping An will never create any new business value again — clearly overly pessimistic. Even conservatively assuming NBV growth of 5%, a reasonable P/EV is at least 0.8–1.0x, implying 30–50% upside.

## 3. Bank of Ningbo (002142.SZ)

**Market cap**: ~CNY 190bn | **PE(TTM)**: ~6.5x | **Dividend yield**: ~3.5%

### Core Investment Thesis

The "outlier" among city commercial banks — the most market-oriented management, with ROE of 12.7% leading peers of similar scale. "Big retail + light capital" strategy is clearly executed; consumer lending and wealth management serve as dual engines. Management stability and execution capability are uniquely strong among city commercial banks.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Management mechanism creates a differentiated competitive advantage. Bank of Ningbo's core moat is not the licence (city commercial bank licences are not scarce) but its "quasi-foreign bank" governance — Singapore's OCBC Bank as the second-largest shareholder; market-rate management compensation; transparent performance appraisal; well-developed talent pipeline. Deep cultivation of the Yangtze River Delta provides regional competitive advantages. On the other hand, city commercial banks face inherent limitations on cross-regional expansion, with a lower scale ceiling than national banks.

### Valuation Assessment

Cheap. PE 6.5x, P/B ~0.9x. For a bank with ROE of 12.7% and growth of 8–10%, valuation offers a clear margin of safety.

## 4. Industrial Bank (601166.SH)

**Market cap**: ~CNY 550bn | **PE(TTM)**: ~5.4x | **P/B**: ~0.51x | **Dividend yield**: ~5.7%

### Core Investment Thesis

One of the most severely below-book joint-stock banks, with P/B of only 0.51x and a 5.7% dividend yield. Mid-transformation under the "commercial bank + investment bank" strategy, focusing on green finance and wealth management. Extremely low valuation provides sufficient margin of safety, but ROE of only 9.2% — below-average profitability among joint-stock banks. Suitable as a high-dividend defensive holding.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Institutional client resources accumulated from historical interbank business represent a differentiated advantage; green finance brand has some industry recognition. On the other hand, Industrial Bank's moat is less clear than CMB's (retail side); interbank business advantages have been eroded by tightening regulation; strategy has pivoted multiple times during the transformation, and a stable new moat has yet to form.

### Valuation Assessment

Extremely cheap, but may be a "value trap." P/B of 0.51x suggests the market doubts net asset quality or believes ROE cannot recover. If ROE stabilises above 10%, the current valuation has clear re-rating potential; if ROE continues to decline, the low valuation is a fair reflection.

## 5. ICBC (601398.SH)

**Market cap**: ~CNY 2,600bn | **PE(TTM)**: ~5.5x | **Dividend yield**: ~4.2%

### Core Investment Thesis

World's largest bank; total assets exceed CNY 48tn. Certainty is extremely high — it cannot fail, pays continuous dividends, and profits are stable. A bond-like equity asset suitable for configurations targeting stable cash flow returns. But growth is limited; net profit growth has long hovered in the low single digits or near zero.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Scale + licence + state ownership create insurmountable barriers. ICBC's market position in corporate banking (especially large infrastructure and state-owned enterprise lending) is irreplaceable. On the other hand, state-owned bank operations are constrained by institutional mechanisms; ROE is trending structurally lower (from 15%+ to ~10%); management decisions prioritise policy objectives over shareholder value maximisation.

### Valuation Assessment

Cheap but limited growth. PE 5.5x, dividend yield 4.2% — fair pricing as "quasi-fixed-income" asset. Neither clearly undervalued nor overvalued.

## 7. East Money Information (300059.SZ)

**Market cap**: ~CNY 302bn | **PE(TTM)**: ~25x | **Profit growth**: +25.8%

### Core Investment Thesis

Internet brokerage leader; the "traffic + licence" dual-barrier model. Eastmoney.com + Tiantian Fund Net have accumulated massive retail investor traffic, then monetised through a brokerage licence. Customer acquisition cost is far below traditional brokerages; marginal cost approaches zero. Net profit growth of 25.8% — highest growth quality in the brokerage industry.

### Economic Moat Analysis

The combination of traffic barrier + licence barrier is unique on A-shares. Eastmoney.com has hundreds of millions of monthly active users; Guba community has high stickiness; user migration costs are high. Fund distribution scale ranks first in the industry (Tiantian Fund Net). On the other hand, performance is highly correlated with market activity — profits surge in bull markets and collapse in bear markets — cyclicality is pronounced. And the internet traffic acquisition model is subject to regulatory policy changes.

### Valuation Assessment

Fair-to-slightly-expensive. PE 25x corresponding to 25.8% growth gives PEG ~1. Given the cyclicality of performance (currently in an active market period), the effective valuation may be higher than the headline number. If market activity cools, profit could retreat 30–40%, causing PE to inflate quickly.

| Moat strength | ★★★★ |
| Management quality | ★★★★ |

**Conclusion**: Unique internet brokerage model; traffic barrier is prominent. Valuation is reasonable but requires active market support. Best researched contrarily during market downturns (i.e., when its profits are at a trough).

## 8. China Life Insurance (601628.SH)

**Market cap**: ~CNY 1,040bn | **PE(TTM)**: ~6.7x | **P/EV**: ~0.72x

### Core Investment Thesis

China's largest life insurer with the highest market share. Long-term beneficiary of population ageing and rising insurance awareness. P/EV of 0.72x is cheap. However, operating efficiency (NBV margin, agent productivity) is systematically weaker than Ping An; the management culture leans SOE, and the pace and depth of reform lag competitors.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Scale + channel + brand form the barrier. Branch network extends to county-level; agent force is largest in scale (though per-agent productivity is low). China Life brand has extremely high recognition in tier-3/4 cities. On the other hand, the insurance industry's moat is better reflected in "management capability" than "scale" — a large but inefficient insurer has a moat that is actually shallow.

### Valuation Assessment

Cheap. P/EV of 0.72x is below 1x, meaning the market assigns low value to future new business. If reforms prove effective and NBV returns to growth, there is re-rating potential. But compared with Ping An (P/EV 0.64x but better operational efficiency), China Life's value proposition is slightly inferior.

## 9. China Pacific Insurance (601601.SH)

P/EV of only 0.5x is the lowest among major insurers — margin of safety is extremely abundant. Life insurance agent transformation is progressing steadily under the "Three Transformations Five Bests" strategy. Extremely cheap. P/EV of 0.5x is the lowest among major listed insurers, meaning the market assigns almost zero or even negative value to CPIC's future new business.
