## 1. Midea Group (000333)

**Market cap**: CNY 550bn | **PE(TTM)**: 12x | **Dividend yield**: 5.6%

### Core Investment Thesis

Global home appliance leader; its diversified platform (home appliances + industrial automation + building technology + robotics) has entered the mature harvest phase. 12x PE + 5.6% dividend yield makes it one of the most attractively priced blue chips on A-shares. A textbook case of great company + great price.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Triple-layered moat of brand + channel + scale: full-category home appliance coverage with the strongest online-offline channels. High degree of globalisation — overseas revenue exceeds 40%. Management professionalism is among the highest in A-shares; the team led by Fang Hongbo demonstrates exceptional execution. The T+3 demand-driven production model and digital capabilities form an efficiency moat.

### Valuation Assessment

12x PE is significantly undervalued; even at a zero-growth mature-stage valuation it would justify 15–18x. The 5.6% dividend yield provides a cushion; downside risk is minimal.

## 2. Gree Electric (000651)

**Market cap**: CNY 224bn | **PE(TTM)**: 7.7x | **Dividend yield**: 9.9%

### Core Investment Thesis

Absolute leader in air conditioners with 35%+ market share; highest profit margin in the industry. 7.7x PE + 9.9% dividend yield makes it highly attractive as a high-yield holding. The core controversy is Dong Mingzhu's management style and failed diversification.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Air conditioner moat is extremely deep: Gree AC holds the top mindshare brand position, with a nationwide dealer network and a comprehensive after-sales service system. Deep technical know-how in AC; energy efficiency ratio continuously leads the industry. However, diversification efforts (mobile phones, NEVs, chips) have repeatedly failed — the moat is limited to the AC business. Channel reform growing pains have passed; online share is rising.

### Valuation Assessment

7.7x PE + nearly 10% dividend yield is highly attractive as a high-dividend asset. But the low valuation partly reflects a market discount for management distrust.

**Conclusion**: Management is the biggest detractor, but 7.7x PE + 10% dividend yield provides an extremely high margin of safety. Suitable as a core holding in a high-dividend portfolio.

## 3. Haier Smart Home (600690)

**Market cap**: CNY 186bn | **PE(TTM)**: 10x

### Core Investment Thesis

The most globally diversified Chinese home appliance company, with overseas revenue at 51%. Global brand matrix including GE Appliances, Fisher & Paykel, and others spans the premium-to-value spectrum. 10x PE is clearly undervaluing its globalisation value.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Global multi-brand matrix: Haier, Casarte (premium), GEA (North America), Fisher & Paykel (premium), Candy (Europe) — strong local operations capability. Smart Home platform strategy creates home appliance interconnectivity and enhances user stickiness. Overseas local production + local branding avoids tariff risk. However, gross margin trails Midea, indicating room for improved operational efficiency.

### Valuation Assessment

10x PE for a globalised home appliance group with 51% overseas revenue is clearly undervalued — comparable Whirlpool carries a higher valuation. Meaningful re-rating potential.

## 4. Hikvision (002415)

**Market cap**: CNY 290bn | **PE(TTM)**: 20x | **Dividend yield**: 2.9%

### Core Investment Thesis

Global security leader (24% market share), transitioning from hardware to an AI vision platform company. Profit growth is reaccelerating; 20x PE is at historical undervaluation. AI empowerment of traditional security is raising ARPU.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Extremely deep technical barriers: full-stack in-house AI algorithm + chip + software + hardware — globally leading in perceptual intelligence. Customers span all tiers — government, enterprise, and SMBs — with an unmatched channel network. Innovative businesses (robotics, automotive electronics, storage, etc.) carve out second curves. US sanctions have paradoxically accelerated domestic substitution; domestic market share rising. Management has a technical background with long-range strategic vision.

### Valuation Assessment

20x PE is at a five-year low; profit growth is reaccelerating to 15–20%, with PEG ~1x — reasonable-to-cheap. If the AI vision transformation succeeds, the valuation centre should shift upward.

## 6. Bull Group (603195)

**Market cap**: CNY 86bn | **PE(TTM)**: 21x | **2026E PE**: 19x

### Core Investment Thesis

Absolute leader in residential electrical products; 60%+ market share in power strips/switches. High gross margin (40%+) + asset-light model + strong brand — the business model is closer to consumer goods than manufacturing. Channel covering one million terminals; extremely deep moat.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Brand mindshare monopoly: consumers' first thought when buying a power strip is Bull — brand equals the category. Channel barrier: one million hardware/convenience store terminal coverage is impossible for latecomers to replicate. Products appear simple but safety certification + quality consistency form barriers. New energy EV charging piles and smart home expansion opens new lanes. The problem is limited growth potential in the traditional business.

### Valuation Assessment

21x PE is reasonable for steady growth (~15%). If 2026E 19x PE materialises, it is attractive. As a steady-state holding, valuation is fair-to-cheap.

## 7. Roborock (688169)

**Market cap**: CNY 35bn | **PE(TTM)**: 18x | **2026E PE**: 16x

### Core Investment Thesis

Top-tier global robot vacuum brand; algorithm and product leadership. Overseas revenue exceeds 60%; brand export overseas has succeeded. However, revenue growth without profit growth is a warning signal — need to determine whether it reflects intensifying competition or intentional margin sacrifice.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Algorithm barrier: LDS laser navigation + AI obstacle avoidance technology leads the industry; best user experience. Brand: has built independent brand recognition overseas (Roborock) without relying on Xiaomi channels. However, competition in robot vacuum is fierce (Dreame, Ecovacs); the technology differentiation window is limited. Products iterate fast; today's advantage may be eroded tomorrow.

### Valuation Assessment

18x PE against a trend of revenue growth without profit growth warrants caution. If profit growth can recover to 20%+, 16x PE is very cheap; otherwise it may be a value trap.

### Core Investment Thesis

## 9. Boss Electric (002508)

**Market cap**: CNY 20.8bn | **PE(TTM)**: 13x

### Core Investment Thesis

Premium kitchen appliance leader with a clear brand positioning. Performance has been pressured by the real estate downturn; but 13x PE already reflects bearish expectations. If real estate bottoms, valuation re-rating opportunity exists.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Premium brand positioning: #1 brand recognition in range hoods; highest average selling price in the industry. Product differentiation: deep accumulation in high-suction + low-noise technology. 13x PE is at a historical trough, already fully reflecting real estate bearishness. If real estate stabilises, re-rating to 18–20x PE offers 40–50% upside. But when real estate troughs is the core uncertainty.

## 10. Dahua Technology (002236)

**Market cap**: CNY 60bn | **PE(TTM)**: 15x

### Core Investment Thesis

Security industry number two; 2025 profit +33% growth is impressive. 15x PE is clearly cheap, but the company perpetually lives in Hikvision's shadow. The core question is "why not just buy Hikvision instead?"

### Economic Moat Analysis

Second-best technical capability in the industry; full product line coverage with differentiated advantages in certain niche scenarios. Rising overseas revenue share offsets domestic competition. However, the gap with Hikvision may widen in the AI era rather than narrow — because AI algorithm R&D requires more data and more investment, and scale disadvantage puts it in an unfavourable position in major project bids.

### Valuation Assessment

15x PE is cheap but has its rationale: number-two discount + visible growth ceiling. As a "cheaper alternative" in the security industry it has allocation value, but not better than buying the leader.
