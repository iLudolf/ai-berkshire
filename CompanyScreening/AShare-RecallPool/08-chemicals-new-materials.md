## 1. Wanhua Chemical (600309.SH)

**Market cap**: ~CNY 280bn | **PE(2026E)**: ~9x | **Dividend yield**: ~3%

### Core Investment Thesis

Global MDI leader with ~30% market share. MDI has only five global suppliers (Wanhua, BASF, Covestro, Huntsman, Dow) — the oligopoly structure is extremely stable. 2026 is a major capacity release year (Penglai and Meishan bases coming onstream); estimated net income of CNY 18.4bn (+28%). Wanhua's core competitiveness lies in continuous technological breakthroughs and cost control — lowest single-tonne MDI cost globally.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Technology + capital + environmental approval form a triple-barrier extremely strong moat. Technical barriers: MDI production processes are extremely complex (involving phosgene reactions with extreme safety requirements); globally only 5 companies have mastered this in 50 years; Wanhua is the only Chinese company that independently developed the complete technology suite. Capital barriers: a new MDI plant costs CNY 5–10bn with a 3–5 year commissioning period. Environmental barriers: given the extreme toxicity of phosgene, new environmental approvals for additional capacity are virtually impossible. On the other hand, MDI downstream markets (refrigerator insulation, automotive, construction) have limited growth (5–7%); Wanhua's high growth relies more on new materials (POE, PMMA, citronellal etc.), and the competitive dynamics of these new businesses are not yet fully established.

### Valuation Assessment

Cheap. PE(2026E) of 9x for a chemical company with ROE 15%+, growth 20%+, and global oligopoly status is extremely attractive. Historical PE centre was 15–20x — the current discount is significant. The market's concerns are chemical cycle downturns and weak real estate demand; but Wanhua is reducing cyclicality through product diversification and globalisation.

**Conclusion**: Strongly recommended for deep research. One of the very few companies globally in the chemical industry with simultaneously "extremely strong moat + excellent management + low valuation." ## 2. Hualu-Hengsheng (600426.SH)

**Market cap**: ~CNY 70bn | **PE(2026E)**: ~9–10x | **Dividend yield**: ~3.5%

### Core Investment Thesis

The "low-cost king" of coal chemicals. Core competitiveness is the flexible production platform — the same equipment can flexibly switch between producing urea, DMF, adipic acid, acetic acid, and other products depending on market prices, always producing the highest-margin product. 2026E net income of CNY 6.8bn (+17%). The Jingzhou base commissioning will open a new growth phase.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Cost advantage + flexible production create a differentiated moat. Hualu-Hengsheng's coal gasification equipment efficiency leads the industry; unit energy consumption is 15–20% below peers — the result of years of process optimisation. The flexible co-production model keeps the company profitable under any chemical product price combination; anti-cyclical capability far exceeds single-product companies. On the other hand, coal chemicals broadly face carbon neutrality policy constraints and long-term transition to green chemicals; and the company's main products (urea, DMF, adipic acid) have lower barriers than Wanhua's MDI, with new entrants continuously emerging.

### Valuation Assessment

Cheap. PE 9–10x, corresponding to ROE 15%+ and stable dividends. In the chemical industry, Hualu-Hengsheng's low-cost advantage allows it to remain profitable even at the cyclical trough (historically never reported a loss) — this certainty deserves a premium that the market has not granted.

**Conclusion**: Warrants deep research. "Cost killer" in coal chemicals; flexible production model is a unique competitive advantage. Valuation is extremely safe; management execution is outstanding.

## 3. Beixin Building Materials (000786.SZ)

**Market cap**: ~CNY 47bn | **PE(TTM)**: ~14.5x | **ROE**: ~20%+

### Core Investment Thesis

Absolute leader in gypsum board with 60%+ domestic market share. Gypsum board competitive dynamics are excellent — Beixin Building Materials dominates; Taishan Gypsum (also under CNBM Group) is ~20%+, creating a de facto monopoly. The unit price of the product is low (a few CNY per square metre) but transport radius is limited (~300km economic radius), naturally forming regional monopolies. ROE consistently above 20%; cash flow is excellent.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Scale + transport radius form an extremely strong moat. Beixin Building Materials has deployed 100+ production lines nationally; each line covers the market within 300km; new entrants even with technology cannot be profitable in areas already served by existing lines (transport costs eat the profit). "Dragon" brand has very high recognition in the decoration market. On the other hand, gypsum board demand is highly correlated with real estate (new-home decoration + existing-home renovation); demand pressure persists in the continuing real estate downturn. The company is seeking a second curve through waterproofing, coatings, and other category expansions — but results are pending validation.

### Valuation Assessment

Fair-to-cheap. PE 14.5x, corresponding to 20%+ ROE and 60%+ market share monopoly, is attractive. Real estate bottoming will be a re-rating catalyst. Historical PE centre was 18–22x — current discount is approximately 30%.

## 4. China Jushi (600176.SH)

**Market cap**: ~CNY 110bn | **PE(2026E)**: ~16–17x

### Core Investment Thesis

Global fiberglass leader with the #1 global production capacity. The fiberglass industry has formed a "Big Three" oligopoly of China Jushi, Taishan Fiberglass, and Chongqing International (CR3 >70%). Downstream benefiting from high-growth sectors: wind turbine blades, automotive lightweighting, AI server PCB boards. Fiberglass prices bottomed in late 2025 and began recovering; 2026 is a cyclical upturn.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Scale + technology + capital form barriers. Fiberglass kilns require large investment (CNY 1bn+ per line), long construction periods (2+ years), and high technical thresholds (kiln design and glass formulation are core know-how). China Jushi leads in high-end products such as E9 ultra-fine yarn and high-modulus fiberglass. Oligopoly structure maintains a floor on pricing competition. On the other hand, fiberglass is meaningfully cyclical; capacity expansion and demand fluctuations cause large price swings. YTD the stock has risen 90%, partially front-running the cycle upturn.

### Valuation Assessment

Reasonable but near-term somewhat expensive. PE(2026E) 16–17x corresponds to cyclical upturn earnings; if 2027 sees industry oversupply and profit decline, the effective PE will inflate. The 90% YTD gain means a heavy profit-taking overhang; pullback risk is real. Great company — but needs a better price.

| Business quality | ★★★★ |
| Moat strength | ★★★★ |
| Management quality | ★★★★ |
| Valuation attractiveness | ★★★ |
| **Overall** | **★★★★** |

**Conclusion**: Global fiberglass leader with good oligopoly structure. But after a 90% YTD rise, near-term valuation is expensive — suggest awaiting a 20–30% pullback before deep research.

## 5. NHU (002001.SZ)

**Market cap**: ~CNY 105bn | **PE(2026E)**: ~10–14x

### Core Investment Thesis

Global vitamin leader (VA global top 3, VE global top 2); simultaneously extending into new materials (PPS, PPA, and other high-performance engineering plastics). Vitamin industry oligopoly structure is stable (only 5–6 major global suppliers with highly concentrated supply). 2026 vitamin prices are in an upswing (DSM exit, BASF capacity incident); new materials business volume is ramping — profit outlook is optimistic.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Technology + environmental approval + oligopoly structure form barriers. Vitamin synthesis routes are complex (VA synthesis exceeds 20 steps); new entrants need 5–10 years R&D. Tightening environmental approvals limit new capacity. Global oligopoly (VA: only DSM, BASF, NHU, Adisseo etc.) has persisted for decades. New materials business (PPS resin) breaks Japan's Toray and DIC monopoly. On the other hand, vitamin prices are strongly cyclical (historical swings of 3–5x); NHU's profit is meaningfully volatile. New materials are still early-stage; whether they can replicate vitamin success remains to be seen.

### Valuation Assessment

Cheap. PE 10–14x (depending on vitamin price assumptions), corresponding to global oligopoly status and new materials second-curve growth — valuation is attractive. Management (founder Hu Baifan family) is low-key and pragmatic with a long-term R&D commitment — a typical "hidden champion" operating philosophy.

## 6. Jinhe Industrial (002597.SZ)

**Market cap**: ~CNY 18bn | **PE(TTM)**: ~15x

### Core Investment Thesis

Leader in sweeteners (acesulfame-K, sucralose); benefiting from the global sugar substitute consumption trend. Sugar substitute penetration in beverages and food is continuously rising (health awareness + sugar reduction policies as dual drivers). Jinhe has 50%+ global market share in acesulfame-K; sucralose is global top three.

### Economic Moat Analysis

Scale advantage and cost advantage in niche segments. Acesulfame-K global market share 50%+ with significant scale effects. However, overall barriers in the sweetener industry are low — domestic sucralose competitors (Jingao Technology etc.) continue to expand capacity, causing industry price wars and margin compression. Newer-generation sweeteners such as erythritol and allulose may substitute traditional products, creating technology path risk. Moat depth is inferior to Wanhua or Hualu-Hengsheng.

### Valuation Assessment

Fair. 15x PE is neither cheap nor expensive. Given intensifying competition and a declining profit trend, the current valuation lacks a clear margin of safety. Need to observe whether the industry price war ends and whether new products (allulose etc.) can open new space.
