## 1. Innolight (300308) — Global Optical Transceiver Leader

### Basic Information

| Metric | Data |
|--------|------|
| Market cap | ~CNY 950–980bn (share price ~CNY 880) |
| PE(TTM) | ~64x |
| Dividend yield | 0.16% |

### Business Quality ★★★★

The business model is relatively clear: providing high-speed optical transceivers for global hyperscale data centres — essentially "selling shovels" into AI infrastructure. Revenue grows alongside AI capex; customers are cloud giants such as Google, Microsoft, and Meta. 2025 net income ~CNY 15bn (estimated); institutional consensus for 2026 is CNY 23–26bn, implying very fast growth.

However: optical transceivers are at heart a manufacturing business with fast technology cycles (400G → 800G → 1.6T); each product generation has a lifecycle of about 2–3 years, requiring continuous R&D to maintain leadership. This is not a simple "invest once, collect forever" business. Gross margin of 30%+ is excellent for hardware manufacturing but nowhere near software or platform companies.

### Moat Strength ★★★★

- **Technology lead**: >50% market share in 800G; 6–12 months ahead of peers in 1.6T; "mass-produce one generation, R&D the next, pre-research the one after" cadence
- **Customer lock-in**: Deeply embedded in Google's OCS ecosystem as a core optical transceiver supplier; high customer switching costs
- **Scale effects**: Largest optical transceiver manufacturer globally; production scale and yield control create cost advantages
- **On the other hand**: Competitors such as Eoptolink are catching up rapidly; the optical transceiver industry has historically seen leadership changes (e.g., Finisar acquired by II-VI); technological barriers are not insurmountable

### Management Quality ★★★★

Controlling shareholder Wang Weixiu (State Council special allowance recipient) stepped back after the 2016 acquisition of Suzhou Xuchuang, ceding day-to-day operations to founder Liu Sheng (Master's from the Chinese Academy of Sciences + PhD from Georgia Tech). This creates a "technical expert + professional manager" governance structure. Management's strategic judgment has been excellent — the 2016 Xuchuang acquisition was a decisive move, and the company continues to invest in CPO and silicon photonics.

### Valuation Attractiveness ★★

Current PE(TTM) ~64x; forward PE for 2026 is ~40–42x. Given profit growth of 120–150%, PEG appears to be 0.3–0.5, which looks "not expensive." But the risks are: cyclical risk in AI capex, historical volatility in the optical transceiver industry (major decline in 2018–2019), and whether the current ~CNY 1tn market cap already fully reflects optimistic expectations.

### Overall Rating ★★★★

### One-Line Summary

Absolute leader in the AI optical transceiver segment; technology lead + customer lock-in form a strong moat, but a CNY 1tn market cap implies very high embedded growth expectations — best monitored for industry panics or downward earnings revisions.

## 2. AMEC (688012) — Etch Equipment

### Basic Information

| Metric | Data |
|--------|------|
| Market cap | ~CNY 200–210bn (share price ~CNY 330) |
| PE(TTM) | ~93x (static); 2026E ~64x |
| Dividend yield | 0.09% |

### Business Quality ★★★★

Semiconductor equipment is the quintessential "sell shovels to gold miners" model. Once a customer qualifies a tool, switching costs are extremely high (requalifying a process takes months to a year). Etch equipment is one of the three core process steps in chip manufacturing, with a large market and high technical barriers. 2025 revenue ~CNY 12.4bn (+37%); net income ~CNY 2.1bn (+31%) — steady growth.

However: the semiconductor equipment industry is meaningfully cyclical, driven by downstream wafer fab capex. And AMEC globally still faces competition from giants such as Lam Research and Tokyo Electron; the overwhelming majority of revenue comes from the domestic market.

### Moat Strength ★★★★

- **Technical barriers**: Etch tool R&D cycles of 5–7 years; AMEC has achieved sub-5nm advanced-node capability and is the only domestic etch tool vendor to have entered tier-one international customers
- **Customer stickiness**: Long tool qualification cycles; once installed in a production line, switching costs are very high
- **Patent moat**: Accumulated a large portfolio of core patents; design choices deliberately differentiated from international peers to avoid infringement while building proprietary barriers
- **On the other hand**: Globally, Lam Research and Tokyo Electron together hold 70%+ of the etch market; AMEC's growth depends heavily on domestic substitution policy and the geopolitical environment

### Management Quality ★★★★★

Founder Dr. Yin Zhiyao (returned to China to start the company at age 60 after 13 years as VP at Applied Materials) is one of the industry's top technical experts. The management team — Du Zhiyou, Ni Tuqiang, and others — are all seasoned international semiconductor equipment veterans. Employee-wide equity incentive programme; core team stability is extremely strong. In 2024, Yin Zhiyao set the ambitious goal of covering 50–60% of critical semiconductor manufacturing equipment categories within 5–10 years, reflecting high strategic vision and execution capability.

### Valuation Attractiveness ★★

PE(TTM) ~93x; even using the consensus 2026E net income of CNY 3.075bn, forward PE is still 64x. Semiconductor equipment is widely recognised as an excellent industry, but the market already ascribes an extreme valuation. Compared with global leader Lam Research at ~25x PE, AMEC's premium reflects the domestic substitution thesis and higher expected growth. If the substitution process slows or the industry cycle turns, valuation faces significant pressure.

### Overall Rating ★★★★

### One-Line Summary

Absolute leader in domestic etch equipment; management is a scarce top-tier technical founding team, but current valuation already fully prices in optimistic domestic substitution expectations — wait for a better price window.

## 3. Montage Technology (688008) — Memory Interface Chips

### Basic Information

| Metric | Data |
|--------|------|
| Market cap | ~CNY 260bn (share price ~CNY 248) |
| PE(TTM) | ~118x (based on EPS CNY 2.11) |
| Dividend yield | 0.28% |

### Business Quality ★★★★★

Memory interface chips are essential components of server memory modules; every DDR5 DIMM requires one RCD chip and multiple data buffer chips. Only two suppliers globally — Montage and Renesas — creating a duopoly. Gross margin exceeds 60%; net margin is 35%, approaching best-in-class levels for fabless chip design. The business model is straightforward: design chips → outsource manufacturing → sell to memory and server vendors.

This is a genuinely "great business": demand grows with server shipment volumes and DDR5 penetration rates, market concentration is extremely high, and product standardisation is high. 2026Q1 net income CNY 847mn (+61% YoY); full-year estimate ~CNY 3bn.

### Moat Strength ★★★★★

- **Duopoly**: Only Montage and Renesas globally can provide a complete DDR5 memory interface chip solution; extremely high barriers
- **Standard setter**: Participates in JEDEC standard development; a key contributor to the DDR5 standard
- **Certification barrier**: Products must pass Intel and AMD platform certification; new entrants face a multi-year qualification process
- **Technology iteration advantage**: Successful DDR4-to-DDR5 transition proves technology continuity
- **On the other hand**: If DDR6 standards cause a technology-route shift, or if Intel/AMD develop in-house interface chips, the duopoly structure could be disrupted

### Management Quality ★★★★

Founder Dr. Yang Chonghe first founded a company in 1997 (acquired by IDT for USD 85mn), then founded Montage in 2004. He has a track record of serial entrepreneurial success and deep semiconductor industry experience. Management philosophy is clear: "anticipate market trends, build a strong team, maintain ample capital." R&D headcount represents 71% of total employees; R&D spending ~15% of revenue (above peers).

Note: Yang Chonghe holds US citizenship (with Chinese permanent residency) — whether this creates potential risk in the current geopolitical environment warrants attention.

### Valuation Attractiveness ★★

Current PE(TTM) ~118x; using 2026E net income of CNY 3bn, forward PE is ~87x. Even at the more optimistic 2027E of CNY 3.8bn, forward PE is still ~68x. A duopoly great business deserves a premium, but current valuation implies that even if profits double over the next two years, PE would still be above 50x. The market is pricing in a very high long-term growth expectation.

### Overall Rating ★★★★

### One-Line Summary

One of two global memory interface chip duopolists; business quality is best among the six (high gross margin + duopoly + standard setting), but valuation is elevated — suitable for long-term tracking with conviction buys on market panics.

## 4. Eoptolink (300502) — High-Speed Optical Transceivers

### Basic Information

| Metric | Data |
|--------|------|
| Market cap | ~CNY 500bn (share price ~CNY 564) |
| PE(TTM) | ~40x (based on 2025 profit ~CNY 9.4–9.8bn) |
| Dividend yield | ~0.1% (estimated) |

### Business Quality ★★★☆

Same segment as Innolight, with a similar business model — providing high-speed optical transceivers for data centres. The difference: Eoptolink focuses on short/medium distance data centre markets, follows the LPO (Linear-drive Pluggable Optics) technology path, and emphasises cost control and vertical integration (covering the full chain from optical chip fabrication to packaging to module assembly).

2025 revenue ~CNY 26.9bn; net income ~CNY 9.4–9.8bn. Institutional 2026E net income: CNY 16.8–17.8bn (+70–80%), very fast growth. Gross margin 38%; net margin 30%+, outstanding within the optical transceiver industry.

However: compared with Innolight's deep customer lock-in, Eoptolink discloses less information about customer concentration and customer mix, and its voice in the high-end 1.6T market may be weaker than Innolight.

### Moat Strength ★★★

- **Vertical integration**: Full-chain coverage from optical chips to modules; clear cost advantage
- **Technology differentiation**: LPO approach offers power and cost advantages for short-distance applications
- **Production capacity scale**: Strong rapid capacity expansion capability
- **On the other hand**: Competition is fierce in the optical transceiver industry; Eoptolink's footprint in the high-end market (1.6T, CPO) is not as deep as Innolight; the founding chairman was penalised for irregular share reductions; a core technical executive had earlier legal disputes — both issues pose a long-term trust concern

### Management Quality ★★★

Chairman Gao Guangrong (vocational school education; started business in 1998; co-founded Eoptolink in 2008). In 2023, the CSRC fined and confiscated CNY 31.49mn for irregular share reductions (concealing family trust shareholding + exceeding reduction limits) — a clear negative mark on management integrity. CEO Huang Xiaolei is a technical expert (Southeast University + University of Electronic Science and Technology Master's) but had an earlier criminal conviction for trade secret theft.

Management's technical execution has been good (impressive multi-generation product iteration from 10G to 800G), but the integrity track record has blemishes. Duan Yongping's standard is "don't do business with dishonest people" — this is a signal worth heeding.

### Valuation Attractiveness ★★★

PE(TTM) ~40x; using 2026E net income of CNY 17bn, forward PE ~29x. Lowest valuation among the three optical transceiver stocks, with comparable growth — offering some valuation attractiveness. But management risk discount and industry cyclicality must be considered.

### Overall Rating ★★★

### One-Line Summary

The number-two optical transceiver contender; vertical integration creates a cost advantage and valuation is relatively reasonable, but management integrity blemishes are a hard flaw — inconsistent with the core principle of "buying a stock is buying the people."

## 5. T&S Communications (300394) — Passive Optical Components

### Basic Information

| Metric | Data |
|--------|------|
| Market cap | ~CNY 310bn (share price ~CNY 400) |
| PE(TTM) | ~110–115x (based on trailing 4-quarter profit ~CNY 2.17bn) |
| Dividend yield | 0.42% |

### Business Quality ★★★★★

T&S Communications' business model is "selling parts" for optical communications: supplying ultra-precision passive optical components and optical engine sub-assemblies to optical transceiver manufacturers upstream. Gross margin 52%; net margin 38% — extremely rare in hardware manufacturing, approaching the levels of fabless chip designers. The business fundamentally builds its moat through ultra-precision manufacturing processes and is a classic "hidden champion."

2025 revenue CNY 5.163bn (+59%); net income CNY 2.017bn (+50%). 2026Q1 net income CNY 492mn (+46%) — growth remains strong. By external sales revenue in 2025, the company holds a 11.7% global market share, ranking first globally among optical component suppliers.

Customer concentration warrants attention: in 2024, Fabrinet alone contributed 61.69% of revenue — extreme concentration that simultaneously proves the company's competitive strength (a top global precision manufacturer chose T&S) and represents a significant risk.

### Moat Strength ★★★★

- **Ultra-precision manufacturing processes**: Core capabilities in precision ceramics, engineering plastics, composite metals, and optical glass — difficult for competitors to replicate quickly
- **Global #1 market share**: 11.7% optical component market share, globally first
- **Customer certification barrier**: Once embedded in a top customer's supply chain, switching costs are high
- **On the other hand**: Extreme customer concentration (single customer >60%) is a clear risk — if Fabrinet redirects orders or diversifies suppliers, results could suffer a major blow

### Management Quality ★★★★

Founders Zou Zhinong (Chairman + Chief Engineer) and Ou Yang (CEO) co-founded the company in 2005. Twenty years of focused specialisation in precision optical components demonstrates the operating philosophy of "focus on doing one thing well." The company has topped the "China Optical Communications Top 10 Most Competitive Enterprises" list for seven consecutive years. Management is stable, focused, and low-profile — consistent with value investors' expectations of excellent management.

The 2026 Hong Kong listing initiative signals proactive capital planning.

### Valuation Attractiveness ★

PE(TTM) as high as 110–115x, at the 94th percentile of historical valuation. Even using 2026E net income of CNY 3.2–3.3bn, forward PE is still ~95x. Using the more optimistic 2027E of CNY 4.1bn, forward PE is still ~75x.

The business quality is genuinely good, but the current price has already fully exhausted two years of future growth. The market offers zero tolerance for error — any quarterly result below expectations could trigger a sharp stock price correction.

### Overall Rating ★★★☆

### One-Line Summary

"Precision manufacturing hidden champion" in optical communications; business quality is excellent (high gross margin + global #1), but a 115x PE implies zero margin of safety — patience required for a cyclical correction or significant earnings release before a reasonable entry.

## 6. Foxconn Industrial Internet (601138) — AI Servers (Passed with Waiver C)

### Basic Information

| Metric | Data |
|--------|------|
| Market cap | ~CNY 1.05tn (share price ~CNY 64) |
| PE(TTM) | ~30x |
| Dividend yield | 1.93% |

### Business Quality ★★★

Foxconn Industrial Internet's business model is fundamentally AI server ODM/OEM manufacturing. Global AI server market share exceeds 40%; it is the core contract manufacturer for Nvidia GB series and Microsoft Azure. 2026Q1 revenue CNY 251.1bn (+57%); net income CNY 10.6bn (+103%) — staggering growth.

Key weakness: gross margin is only 7%; net margin only 3.8% (which is why the quality elimination screen required a "Waiver C" to pass). This is a classic "big but not fat" business — revenues at the trillion-yuan scale but extremely thin margins. Each server generates little profit; winning requires massive shipment volumes. If downstream demand fluctuates or customers press on price, profit elasticity is limited.

### Moat Strength ★★★

- **Scale barrier**: Annual capacity exceeding 2 million AI servers — globally unrivalled
- **Deep customer integration**: Joint development from early design phase; top five customers contribute >60% of revenue
- **Global footprint**: Multi-country production bases diversify geopolitical risk
- **Yield and delivery capability**: Yield control in large-scale production is the core competitive advantage
- **On the other hand**: Contract manufacturing inherently lacks pricing power; core profits are captured by brand owners (Nvidia, cloud vendors); 7% gross margin means almost no bargaining power; if competitors (Wistron, Quanta) expand capacity aggressively, margins could compress further

### Management Quality ★★★

Backed by the Foxconn/Hon Hai system with global manufacturing management experience. However, as a listed vehicle of the Foxconn group, independence is in question — corporate governance is significantly influenced by the parent. The strong management style of the Terry Gou era ensured execution, but it also means major decisions may serve group interests rather than A-share minority shareholders.

### Valuation Attractiveness ★★★★

PE(TTM) ~30x; using 2026E net income of CNY 51.5bn, forward PE ~21x. Lowest valuation among the six companies, with a ~2% dividend yield. For a company with revenues at the trillion-yuan scale and profit growth of 45%+, a 21x forward PE looks reasonable or even cheap.

But low valuation has its logic: a 3.8% net margin means the contract manufacturing nature is unchanged, and long-term ROE ceiling is limited.

### Overall Rating ★★★

### One-Line Summary

Absolute scale king of global AI server contract manufacturing; valuation is reasonable with a dividend, but the 7% gross margin contract-manufacturing nature means it "earns the hard way" — does not meet the highest standard of a "great business."
