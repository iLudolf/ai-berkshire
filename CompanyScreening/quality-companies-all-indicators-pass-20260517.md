## Indicator Framework

| # | Indicator | Elimination Condition |
|---|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | 10-year average ROE | < 8% |
| 2 | 5-year cumulative free cash flow | Negative |
| 3 | Interest coverage ratio | < 2x |
| 4 | Long-term gross margin | < 15% |
| 5 | Operating cash flow / Net income | < 0.7 |
| 6 | Long-term net profit margin | < 5% |
| 7 | 5-year share dilution | > 20% |

Exemption A (Indicator 1): Listed < 10 years + gross margin > 30% + positive operating cash flow in last 2 years
Exemption B (Indicator 6): Gross margin > 30% + net profit margin > 5% in last 2 years or on an upward trend

## Common Characteristics of These Companies

1. **All gross margins > 30%** — Lowest is Meituan at 35%, highest is Meta at 81%
2. **All ROEs > 12%** — Indicates capital efficiency far exceeding opportunity cost
3. **All free cash flows are positive** — Earnings are real cash, not accounting profits
4. **None dilute shareholders** — Most are buying back and reducing share count

This is the financial profile of a "first-class quality company": high gross margin (pricing power) + high ROE (capital efficiency) + strong cash flow (earnings quality) + no dilution (respect for shareholders).
