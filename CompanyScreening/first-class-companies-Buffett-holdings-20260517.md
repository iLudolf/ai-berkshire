### Summary of Financial Characteristics of Buffett's Stock Selection

1. **High ROE is the core screening criterion**: Companies that passed generally have 10-year average ROE above 15%; top holdings (Apple, Moody's, AXP) exceed 25%. Only OXY and PetroChina are exceptions — both were "bargain speculation" rather than typical long-term Buffett-style holdings.

2. **Free cash flow is almost universally positive**: Buffett very rarely buys companies with negative cash flow. The only exception is bottom-fishing at cyclical troughs (e.g., OXY in 2020).

3. **Strong preference for high gross margins**: Apple (46%), Coca-Cola (62%), Moody's (74%), VeriSign (88%), American Express (63%). Buffett's favorite companies tend to have gross margins above 40%, reflecting strong pricing power.

4. **Net margin > 10% is the norm**: Most core holdings have net margins between 10% and 30%. Kroger (1.8%) and Nucor (5.4%) are rare low-net-margin exceptions with correspondingly smaller position sizes.

5. **Buybacks shrink share count rather than dilute**: Almost all holdings are buying back shares — share counts over five years have not expanded but actually contracted. This is a capital allocation approach Buffett explicitly prefers.

6. **Ample interest coverage**: Excluding financial companies, all holdings have interest coverage > 5x, with most > 10x. Buffett has an extreme aversion to debt risk.

7. **Patterns in failures / borderline cases**:
   - Kraft Heinz: High-priced acquisition + goodwill impairments = what Buffett himself called "a mistake"
   - OXY: A special "buy cheap" opportunity, not a typical long-term holding
   - Cyclical industries (energy, steel) inherently struggle to meet the stability requirements for ROE and net margin

**Core Conclusion**: Buffett's holdings are highly concentrated in companies with "high ROE + high gross margin + strong cash flow + low leverage + sustained buybacks." These companies typically possess powerful economic moats (brands, network effects, switching costs) that allow them to sustain excess returns over the long term. The seven-criterion quality elimination screen effectively identifies the core logic behind his holdings.
