# STAR Market Top 100 Companies: Quality Elimination Screen Report

> Screen Date: May 17, 2026
> Data Sources: East Money, Company Annual/Quarterly Reports (based on each company's 2025 annual report data)
> Screen Criteria: 7 quality elimination indicators + 2 exemption rules (v2)

---

## Indicator Framework

| # | Indicator | Elimination Condition |
|---|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | 10-year average ROE | < 8% |
| 2 | 5-year cumulative free cash flow | Negative |
| 3 | Interest coverage ratio | < 2x |
| 4 | Long-term gross margin | < 15% |
| 5 | Operating cash flow / Net income | < 0.7 |
| 6 | Long-term net margin | < 5% |
| 7 | 5-year share count expansion | > 20% |

Exemption A (Indicator 1): Listed < 10 years + gross margin > 30% + positive operating cash flow in past 2 years
Exemption B (Indicator 6): Gross margin > 30% + net margin > 5% or rising trend in past 2 years

## Core Conclusions

After quality elimination screening of the STAR Market top 100 companies:
- **Pass rate 27%** — only 27 out of 100 companies passed all 7 indicators
- **Semiconductor equipment is the biggest winner** — 7 companies passed, accounting for 26%, reflecting the superiority of the "picks and shovels" business model
- **Losses are the biggest killer** — 56% of companies were eliminated due to losses or just turning profitable, reflecting poor overall earnings quality on the STAR Market
- **High gross margin = high pass rate** — the median gross margin of the 27 passing companies is approximately 45%, far higher than eliminated companies

This is highly consistent with Buffett's judgment: **truly good businesses are those that have already proven they can earn money consistently — not stories about companies that "might make money in the future."**
