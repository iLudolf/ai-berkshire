## I. Business Model

- **Food delivery**: High-frequency need, core foundation — earns commission + advertising fees
- **Meituan Flash (Instant commerce)**: Leverages food delivery rider network to compress "next-day delivery" to under one hour, opening new market space
- **In-store & hotel/travel**: Low-frequency, high-margin; traffic directed from food delivery
- Overall positioning: Urban life infrastructure

---

## II. Economic Moat

**Three-sided network effect** (users × merchants × riders):

- More users → more merchants → more riders → richer selection, lower delivery costs → better experience → more users
- All three sides mutually locked in; any one side is difficult to migrate away

**Stress test already passed**:
- Ele.me burned ~RMB 50 billion and failed to break through
- The current food delivery war (JD + Alibaba) is estimated to have burned RMB 88–90 billion; Meituan's share declined somewhat but the three-sided network remains intact
- After cumulative RMB 100+ billion in stress tests, the moat still stands — a de facto monopoly has formed
- Conclusion: The moat has **widened** over the past five years, not narrowed

**The food delivery war is irrational**: Alibaba's core is e-commerce + cloud + AI; synergies with food delivery are questionable. If synergies prove illusory, Alibaba will reduce investment just as it did with Ele.me. Current signals suggest the war has been called off. Whatever the outcome, it is favorable for Meituan.
